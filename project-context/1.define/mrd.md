# Market Research Document (MRD)
## Recruitment Assistant

## 1. Problem Statement
Recruitment teams often spend significant time sourcing, screening, and comparing candidates manually. This creates delays, inconsistent evaluation criteria, and difficulty scaling hiring workflows when there are many applicants or open roles.

## 2. Target Users
The primary users are recruiters who need to identify qualified candidates quickly. Secondary users include hiring managers who need structured candidate recommendations and HR teams managing high-volume recruitment pipelines.

## 3. Market Opportunity
A recruitment assistant can reduce manual sourcing effort, improve consistency in candidate evaluation, and help teams prioritize the strongest candidates faster. AI agents can support repetitive research and evaluation tasks while recruiters remain responsible for final judgment.

## 4. Competitive Landscape
Traditional applicant tracking systems help store and manage candidate data, but they often require manual review and comparison. The opportunity for a multi-agent recruitment assistant is to add intelligent sourcing, evaluation, and recommendation capabilities on top of structured hiring criteria.

## 5. Value Proposition
The recruitment assistant helps hiring teams save time, improve candidate matching, and make more consistent recommendations by coordinating specialized AI agents for research, evaluation, and ranking.
